More and more studies are using long-read sequencing, often with other technologies, to produce high-quality catalogs of structural variation (SV). Many novel SVs are being discovered and most of them are sequence-resolved. For example:

  • Audano et al. Cell 2019.
  • The Genome in a Bottle consortium.
  • The Human Genome Structural Variation Consortium.

Are they consistent with each other or are there signs of errors in their definition?

Human Genome Structural Variation Consortium

I downloaded the merged PhasedSV and MsPAC callset as described in Chaisson et al. bioRxiv 2018. They provide a callset for each of the three individuals studied: HG00514 (Han chinese), HG00733 (Puerto Ricans), and NA19240 (YRI Nigerian).

Size distribution

It’s good to keep in mind that the catalog from Audano et al. is based on 15 individuals which might explain some of the differences in size distribution. Rare variants will be enriched in this catalog, and rare variants tend to be smaller.
